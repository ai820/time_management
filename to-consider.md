# Strategies to Consider

> Review this list periodically. Try one or two new strategies at a time - don't overwhelm yourself.

---

## Study Techniques

### Active Learning
- [ ] **Active recall** - Test yourself instead of passively re-reading notes
- [ ] **Feynman technique** - Explain concepts in simple terms as if teaching a child
- [ ] **Practice questions first** - Attempt problems before reading the solution/theory
- [ ] **Closed-book summaries** - After studying a topic, write everything you remember without notes
- [ ] **Teach someone else** - Explain topics to friends, family, or even an imaginary student
- [ ] **Question generation** - Create your own exam questions while studying

### Spaced Repetition & Memory
- [ ] **Spaced repetition apps** - Anki for definitions (Economics, Business), formulas (Maths)
- [ ] **Review schedule** - Day 1, Day 3, Day 7, Day 14, Day 30
- [ ] **Interleaving** - Mix different topics/subjects in one study session instead of blocking
- [ ] **Memory palace** - Visualise information in familiar locations for recall
- [ ] **Mnemonics** - Create acronyms, rhymes, or stories for lists/processes
- [ ] **Sleep on it** - Review difficult material right before bed for memory consolidation

### Practice & Application
- [ ] **Past papers under timed conditions** - Simulate real exam pressure
- [ ] **Mark your own work** - Use marking guidelines to understand how marks are allocated
- [ ] **Redo incorrect questions** - Don't just read the solution, actually redo it
- [ ] **Vary problem types** - Don't just do easy questions; include challenging ones
- [ ] **Blind problem solving** - Attempt without hints or formula sheets first

## Note-Taking & Organisation

### Note Methods
- [ ] **Cornell method** - Divide page into notes, cues, and summary sections
- [ ] **Mind maps** - Visual connections between concepts (great for Business/Economics)
- [ ] **Outline method** - Hierarchical bullet points for structured subjects
- [ ] **Flow notes** - Connect ideas with arrows showing relationships
- [ ] **Sketch notes** - Combine drawings with text for visual memory

### Consolidation Tools
- [ ] **One-page summaries** - Condense each topic to a single page
- [ ] **Formula sheets** - Create and memorise key formulas (Maths)
- [ ] **Quote/technique banks** - Collect quotes and literary techniques (English)
- [ ] **Definition glossaries** - Key terms with concise definitions (Economics, Business)
- [ ] **Process flowcharts** - Step-by-step diagrams for methods (Maths problem types)
- [ ] **Comparison tables** - Side-by-side comparisons of similar concepts

### Error Tracking
- [ ] **Error log/journal** - Record mistakes with the concept and correct method
- [ ] **Categorise mistakes** - Silly errors vs conceptual gaps vs time pressure
- [ ] **Review error log before exams** - Focus on your personal weak spots
- [ ] **Track question types** - Note which question styles you struggle with

### Digital Organisation
- [ ] **Cloud backup** - Google Drive/OneDrive for notes (never lose work)
- [ ] **Consistent file naming** - Subject_Topic_Date format
- [ ] **Bookmark key resources** - Organise useful websites by subject
- [ ] **Digital flashcard decks** - Organised by subject and topic

## Health & Energy

### Sleep
- [ ] **Consistent sleep schedule** - Same bedtime and wake time daily
- [ ] **7-9 hours minimum** - Non-negotiable for memory and focus
- [ ] **No screens 30-60 min before bed** - Blue light disrupts sleep quality
- [ ] **No all-nighters** - Diminishing returns; sleep beats cramming
- [ ] **Power naps** - 10-20 min max if needed (not after 3pm)

### Physical Health
- [ ] **Regular exercise** - Already doing gym; keep it up
- [ ] **Hydration** - Keep water bottle at desk; dehydration = brain fog
- [ ] **Healthy snacks while studying** - Nuts, fruit, not sugar crashes
- [ ] **Stretch breaks** - Stand up, move every hour
- [ ] **Eye breaks** - 20-20-20 rule (every 20 min, look 20 feet away for 20 sec)

### Mental Breaks
- [ ] **Pomodoro technique** - 25 min work / 5 min break
- [ ] **50/10 splits** - 50 min work / 10 min break for deeper focus
- [ ] **Scheduled downtime** - Guilt-free relaxation is productive
- [ ] **Change of scenery** - Study in different locations occasionally
- [ ] **Nature/walks** - Brief outdoor time resets focus

## Mindset & Motivation

### Goal Setting
- [ ] **Process goals > outcome goals** - "Do 2 past papers" not "get 90%"
- [ ] **Weekly goals** - Specific, achievable targets each week
- [ ] **Daily MIT (Most Important Task)** - Identify the one thing that matters most
- [ ] **Celebrate small wins** - Acknowledge progress to maintain motivation
- [ ] **Visualise success** - Imagine achieving your goals concretely

### Dealing with Challenges
- [ ] **Growth mindset** - "I can't do this yet" instead of "I can't do this"
- [ ] **Reframe difficulty** - Hard topics = higher growth potential
- [ ] **Start with 5 minutes** - Overcome procrastination by committing to just start
- [ ] **Forgive bad days** - One unproductive day doesn't ruin everything
- [ ] **Identify avoidance patterns** - Notice when/why you procrastinate

### Stress Management
- [ ] **Breathing exercises** - 4-7-8 breathing or box breathing
- [ ] **Brain dumps** - Write out all worries/tasks to clear mental load
- [ ] **Worst-case thinking** - "What's the actual worst outcome?" (usually manageable)
- [ ] **Talk to someone** - Friends, family, school counsellor
- [ ] **Perspective check** - HSC matters, but it's not everything

## Social & Collaborative Learning

### Study Groups
- [ ] **Form a study group** - 2-4 people, same subjects
- [ ] **Teach each other** - Take turns explaining topics
- [ ] **Quiz each other** - Verbal testing in groups
- [ ] **Share resources** - Notes, past papers, tips
- [ ] **Accountability buddies** - Check in on each other's progress

### Using Teachers
- [ ] **Ask questions in class** - Don't stay confused
- [ ] **Visit during free periods** - One-on-one help
- [ ] **Request feedback on practice responses** - Especially for English/Business
- [ ] **Ask for extra resources** - Teachers often have more past papers/notes
- [ ] **Clarify marking criteria** - Understand exactly what earns marks

### Online Resources
- [ ] **YouTube channels** - Eddie Woo (Maths), Economics Explained, etc.
- [ ] **Past paper databases** - NESA, school archives
- [ ] **Reddit/Discord communities** - r/HSC, subject-specific servers
- [ ] **Online tutoring** - If budget allows and needed

## Exam-Specific Strategies

### Before the Exam
- [ ] **Mark scheme analysis** - Understand how marks are allocated
- [ ] **Identify high-yield topics** - Focus on frequently tested areas
- [ ] **Create exam checklists** - What to bring, what to review
- [ ] **Simulate exam conditions** - Full papers, timed, no distractions
- [ ] **Prepare equipment** - Pens, calculator, ID ready the night before

### During the Exam
- [ ] **Read the whole paper first** - Plan time allocation
- [ ] **Allocate time by marks** - Roughly 1 mark = 1-1.5 minutes
- [ ] **Answer easy questions first** - Build confidence, secure marks
- [ ] **Show all working (Maths)** - Partial marks for method
- [ ] **Plan extended responses** - Quick dot-point plan before writing
- [ ] **Leave time to review** - 5-10 min at end to check answers
- [ ] **Don't leave blanks** - Always attempt something

### After the Exam
- [ ] **Don't post-mortem excessively** - Move on to the next one
- [ ] **Review returned papers** - Learn from mistakes for future exams
- [ ] **Update error log** - Add new mistake patterns

## Subject-Specific Ideas

### Maths (Advanced, Ext 1, Ext 2)
- [ ] **Formula memorisation** - Know them cold before exam
- [ ] **Recognise problem types** - Identify the method needed quickly
- [ ] **Check answers by substitution** - Verify solutions where possible
- [ ] **Draw diagrams** - Visualise problems, especially in calculus/geometry
- [ ] **Understand proofs** - Don't just memorise; know why they work
- [ ] **Build difficulty gradually** - Master basics before extension

### Economics
- [ ] **Current events** - Read news; use real-world examples
- [ ] **Diagram practice** - Draw and label key graphs from memory
- [ ] **Link concepts** - Show how topics connect (e.g., inflation to interest rates)
- [ ] **Statistics bank** - Keep updated stats for essays
- [ ] **Essay structures** - PEEL paragraphs, clear arguments

### Business Studies
- [ ] **Case study bank** - Collect real business examples
- [ ] **Syllabus dot points** - Ensure every point is covered
- [ ] **Stimulus response practice** - Analyse business scenarios
- [ ] **Link theory to practice** - Always use business examples
- [ ] **Key terms** - Define precisely using syllabus language

### English Standard
- [ ] **Text knowledge** - Know your texts deeply
- [ ] **Quote bank** - Memorise key quotes with techniques
- [ ] **Technique identification** - Practice spotting and naming techniques
- [ ] **Essay planning speed** - Quick plans under exam pressure
- [ ] **Rubric familiarity** - Know exactly what markers want
- [ ] **Handwriting practice** - Legibility matters under time pressure
- [ ] **Read widely** - Broader vocabulary and ideas

## Environment & Tools

### Study Space
- [ ] **Dedicated study area** - Same place signals "study mode"
- [ ] **Clean desk** - Clutter = distraction
- [ ] **Good lighting** - Natural light or bright lamp
- [ ] **Comfortable (not too comfortable)** - Chair and desk, not bed
- [ ] **Temperature control** - Not too hot or cold

### Minimising Distractions
- [ ] **Phone in another room** - Or use app blockers (Forest, Cold Turkey)
- [ ] **Website blockers** - Block social media during study time
- [ ] **Noise management** - Silence, white noise, or lo-fi music
- [ ] **Tell others your study times** - Reduce interruptions
- [ ] **"Do not disturb" mode** - On all devices

### Tools & Apps
- [ ] **Anki** - Spaced repetition flashcards
- [ ] **Notion/Obsidian** - Note organisation
- [ ] **Forest** - Phone lock with gamification
- [ ] **Toggl** - Time tracking to see where time goes
- [ ] **Google Calendar** - Block out study sessions

## Weekly Habits to Build

- [ ] **Sunday planning session** - Plan the week ahead
- [ ] **Daily review** - 10 min at end of day: what did I learn?
- [ ] **Weekly reflection** - What worked? What to adjust?
- [ ] **Monthly goal check** - Are you on track for bigger goals?
- [ ] **Regular breaks** - Scheduled rest prevents burnout

---

## Things to Avoid

- [ ] **Passive re-reading** - Feels productive but isn't
- [ ] **Highlighting everything** - Be selective
- [ ] **Multitasking** - Focus on one thing at a time
- [ ] **Comparing to others** - Focus on your own progress
- [ ] **Perfectionism** - Done is better than perfect
- [ ] **Cramming** - Distributed practice beats last-minute panic
- [ ] **Ignoring weak areas** - Face them; don't avoid
- [ ] **Overloading schedule** - Burnout is real
